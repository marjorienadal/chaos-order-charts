This Time is Different: Input Shock Breadth
2008 was a single input shock. 2026 is four simultaneous ones. The cascade is slower to absorb and harder to reverse.
2008
Food crisis
1 input disrupted
Corn — ethanol mandate
US ethanol policy pulled corn away from feed. Drought compounded the pressure.
2026
Hormuz crisis
4 inputs disrupted simultaneously
Nitrogen — urea and ammonia
34–49% of global Gulf exports blocked. India CFR urea +70%. Bangladesh plants dark.
Phosphate — sulfur feedstock
44% of seaborne sulfur originates in the Gulf. Morocco's OCP needs 6.7m tonnes/year.
Natural gas — fertiliser feedstock
LNG from Qatar's North Field disrupted. Plants shutting not on price but on supply.
Energy — diesel and transport
European diesel futures +81% in March. Direct cost transmission to farm operations.
In 2008, one input shock drove food riots in Port-au-Prince, Cairo and Dhaka. The 2026 shock hits four inputs at once, making substitution harder and the recovery path more brittle. The cascade is not faster — it is deeper.
