The Central Bank Dilemma
No clean exit exists
Option A
Cut
Signals tolerance for above-target inflation precisely when a supply shock is transmitting through the price level. Incompatible with credibility rebuilt at significant cost since 2022.
Destroys credibility
Option B
Hike
Accelerates demand destruction into an economy already losing momentum. Brings forward the recession central banks have spent three years trying to avoid.
Triggers recession
Option C
Hold
The least bad option. But holding into slowing growth with above-target inflation is stagflation by another name — and the option markets have not yet fully priced.
Underpriced by markets
1973 parallel: The first oil shock produced policy error not because central bankers were incompetent, but because the shock was stagflationary by nature. The tools are the same in 2026. The margin for error is smaller.

Three Shocks, Three Speeds
Why this isn't over when oil settles
The three shocks from Operation Epic Fury are not sequential — they are simultaneous but arrive at different speeds. The energy shock is already in the price. The fertiliser and freight shocks are not yet fully priced because their CPI transmission is months away.
NOW
Feb 28 – Mar 10
Energy shock · Rate repricing
Brent +$20, TTF +70%, gilt market repricing
Commodity price move visible immediately. UK 10-year gilt records one of its largest weekly moves since September 2022. Rate cut consensus broken.
LIVE — already in the price
APR – MAY
6–8 weeks out
Energy → CPI transmission
March & April G7 CPI prints confirm the pass-through
For a late-February shock, March data shows the initial impact; April confirms it. February CPI will show essentially none of it. This is where consensus gets revised.
BUILDING — not yet in forecasts
APR – MAY
Q1 earnings
Freight → corporate margins
Q1 earnings season: guidance cuts and margin misses
Cape of Good Hope rerouting adds ~10–14 days per cargo. Permanent per-unit cost increase on ~15% of global container trade. Appears in Q1 guidance before aggregate data confirms it.
BUILDING — underpriced
JUL – SEP
4–6 months out
Fertiliser → food prices
Food CPI spike: spring planting decisions priced in
>30% of globally traded urea transits Hormuz. Urea spot prices +~30% week one. Fertiliser shocks do not register immediately — they reach food prices 4–6 months later. Central bank models are likely to be behind this.
MOST UNDERESTIMATED SHOCK
Q2 2026
April – June
Credit market stress
Private credit covenant stress surfaces
Rising input costs hit revenue. Tighter financial conditions hit refinancing. The two arrive simultaneously. Lender communications, amendments, and restructuring announcements begin to appear.
STRUCTURAL — building now
Q3 – Q4
2026
Refinancing wall
$10 trillion refinancing wall meets no central bank cushion
The wall identified in August 2025 now faces a world where the rate cut path has been removed. Companies underwritten at 2020–2022 rates refinancing into a world where investment grade clears in the mid-5s to mid-6s and high yield starts with an 8.
STRUCTURAL — no policy offset available
