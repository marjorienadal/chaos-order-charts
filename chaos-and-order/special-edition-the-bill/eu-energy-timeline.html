European Energy Crisis · Transmission Timeline

A Calendar of Consequences

From market shock to physical shortage — the lag that ends in late April
28 Feb 2026 Day zero
Hormuz effectively closed to energy traffic
Iranian restrictions reduce tanker transits by over 90%. Last loaded tankers depart the Gulf.
10 Apr 2026 Week 6
ACI Europe: jet fuel shortage within 3 weeks
Industry body writes to EU Transport Commissioner. Strategic reserves at 8–10 days at major airports.
Late Apr 2026 Week 7–8
Last pre-war tankers reach European refineries
Pre-war supply buffer exhausted. Refineries drawing on reserves only. War premium becomes consumer price.
25 Apr 2026 Policy trigger
EU short-term Russian LNG contracts begin phasing out
Phase-out begins as Europe pays $3.3bn for Yamal LNG in Q1 — 97% of available cargoes.
Apr–Oct 2026 Peak pressure
Consumer price transmission window
12–18 month commodity lag peaks. Livestock liquidation lag converges simultaneously. Household impact fully visible.
1 Nov 2026 EU deadline
EU 90% storage regulatory target
Requires injecting ~61 percentage points from April's 28.9%. Cost: €35–44bn. Qatar still offline.
End 2027 Full ban
Full EU ban on Russian LNG imports
The supply Europe is banning is the supply Europe currently cannot survive without.
The piece is not about whether an energy crisis exists. It is about when the lag ends — the point at which market risk becomes physical shortage. That moment is late April 2026.
