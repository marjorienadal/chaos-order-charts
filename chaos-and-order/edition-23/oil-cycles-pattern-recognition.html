Each major bottom showed defensive positioning by producers (cost cuts, production restraint, restructuring) 6-12 months BEFORE the price recovery began. We're seeing those defensive signals now in Q4 2025, but the setup isn't complete yet.

What 2025 Shares With Past Cycles

1998-99 ($10 oil): OPEC cuts, Asian demand collapse, producer capitulation. Recovery began 6-9 months after the bottom as supply discipline took hold and Asian economies stabilized.

2015-16 ($30 oil): U.S. shale glut, Saudi market share war, producer bankruptcies. Recovery began 8-12 months after bottom as U.S. production finally rolled over and OPEC implemented cuts.

2020 ($20 oil): COVID demand destruction, storage crisis, negative WTI. Recovery began 6-9 months after bottom as vaccines emerged and OPEC+ maintained historic production cuts.

2025 ($60 oil): China artificial demand rolling off, FCF deterioration, defensive positioning. TotalEnergies merger, OPEC+ restraint at $60-65, majors cutting costs. The defensive actions are appearing NOW, but unlike past cycles we're not at maximum despair pricing yet.

The Critical Difference: Previous cycles bottomed at $10-30 (genuine despair). Current price of $60 is compressed but not collapsed.
