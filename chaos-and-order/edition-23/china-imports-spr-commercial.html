The Decomposition

IEA Q3 Headline: 920k bpd year-over-year growth, led by China. Sounds strong.

Reality: That 920k bpd headline minus 500-700k bpd of SPR stockbuilding gets you into the low hundreds for actual commercial demand growth. EIA estimates strategic inventory builds averaged ~800k bpd Jan-Sep 2025. S&P Global pegs it at ~530k bpd average through 2025.

What's Left: Columbia SIPA's compilation of major agency estimates for China 2024-2025 demand growth ranges from -242k to +320k bpd. My read: 2025 commercial demand is somewhere in the 0-300k bpd range, not the 920k headline.

Structural Trends: Transportation fuel consumption is 2.5% below 2021 peak levels (EVs, LNG trucks, high-speed rail eating into diesel/gasoline). Petrochemical feedstock growth is strong but doesn't fully offset the transportation decline.

The Problem: SPR stockpiling is non-recurring demand brought forward from future years. It has a visible expiration date. When those strategic reserves fill in early 2026, that 500-800k bpd artificial support disappears. The market will have to price off actual consumption growth—which is structurally weak.

Sources: EIA estimates for China SPR builds (Jan-Sep 2025 average ~800k bpd), S&P Global Commodity Insights (~530k bpd average 2025), IEA Oil Market Report November 2025 (920k bpd YoY growth), Columbia SIPA Center on Global Energy Policy demand estimates compilation (-242k to +320k bpd range).
