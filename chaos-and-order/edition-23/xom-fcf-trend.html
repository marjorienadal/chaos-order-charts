The Floor Is Cracking

Original Thesis: Entry at $60-65 oil was based on integrated majors generating $35-40B FCF, providing downside protection. ExxonMobil's 2024 actual of $34.4B was already at the bottom of that range.

Current Reality: 2025 is tracking toward high-20s to low-30s FCF—below the floor. The deterioration comes from lower average oil prices vs 2024, margin compression, and elevated capex. This isn't just market conditions; it's the business generating less cash at similar price levels.

Why This Matters: If XOM can't hold $34B+ FCF at $60-65 oil, the original entry thesis breaks. The "margin of safety" evaporates. This is why deployment is deferred to April-May 2026, when Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 earnings will show whether the floor stabilizes or continues cracking.

Deployment Requirement: Need to see evidence that FCF stabilizes at $34B+ (2024 actual level) before committing capital. Otherwise, you're catching a falling knife with no floor in sight.
