What This Tells You

April 2020 (Red Line): The world literally ran out of storage space. WTI went negative, Brent crashed to $20s with 12-month contracts at $40+. This was a storage crisis—traders were paying people to take oil. The steep upward curve (super contango) signaled maximum panic and oversupply.

December 2025 (Blue Line): Current curve is essentially flat with mild backwardation ($62.49 front month → $61.62 in 12 months). This signals balanced markets—not abundant enough for contango storage arbitrage, not scarce enough for backwardation premiums.

Investment Implication: We're not in 2020-style collapse territory. We're in neutral structure, which supports the decision to defer deployment until we see either: (a) despair pricing ($50-60) with maintained OPEC+ discipline, or (b) clear demand recovery justifying current prices.

Pattern Recognition: What matters is that defensive positioning by majors (cost cuts, TotalEnergies merger, OPEC+ restraint) is starting to mirror late-cycle capitulation seen 6-12 months before recoveries in 1998-99, 2015-16, and 2020. The curve structure confirms we're not there yet—but the setup is forming.
