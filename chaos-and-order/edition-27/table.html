When Meat Becomes a Luxury: The Cascade Timeline
From Hormuz closure to political consequences — six phases, three years
Phase Trigger Timeline Market impact
Fertiliser shock Hormuz closure cuts urea, ammonia and sulfur supply simultaneously Now 34–49% of Gulf fertiliser exports disrupted. Plants across Bangladesh dark. Thirty ammonia and urea facilities in India shut down or curtailed. Natural gas feedstock not arriving.
Planting window US Corn Belt plants April–May. Prevent-plant insurance deadline late May. Apr–May 2026 After late May, US corn yield damage locked regardless of Hormuz resolution. India kharif season opens with the monsoon in June — 25% of global crop weight. Goes in with whatever fertiliser is or is not available.
Cereal price spike Channel inventory depletes. Fertiliser shortage transmits to yield. Q3–Q4 2026 Base case (40% probability): +63% cereal prices, 6% global yield loss. High case (55–60% probability): +120% cereal prices, 12% yield loss. US NOLA granular urea already +76% toward 2022 peak.
Livestock liquidation Feed costs exceed animal value. Rational response: sell while the animals still have value. Q4 2026–Q1 2027 Brief counterintuitive meat surplus as herds are culled. Prices temporarily soft. Then supply craters as liquidated herds are not replaced. Cattle rebuild minimum: 30 months. Full cattle cycle: 8–12 years.
Meat price spike Supply crater after liquidation. No herd to replace. Q1–Q3 2027 CME Live Cattle and Lean Hog futures not yet pricing this scenario. US cattle herd at 1950s lows entering 2026 after seven consecutive years of contraction. CF Industries, Nutrien, UAN Q1 earnings guidance is the signal to watch.
Political consequences Protein unaffordable for Western middle class. Government intervention reflex activates. Late 2027 France: bread price controls discussed for first time since 1987. Thailand, India, Argentina export restriction precedents. Central bank rate cut pressure becomes overwhelming. Debasement thesis stops being theoretical.
